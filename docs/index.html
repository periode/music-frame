pogłos
pogłos
  1. an acoustic phenomenon consisting in the prolongation of sound, caused by the action of reflected waves in a closed room
  2. a multiplication of sound
  3. a reference to something, especially in someone's work

idea

pogłos is a generative sonic atmosphere contained in a wooden frame.

it is slightly bigger than an A3 piece of paper, and it consists of a small computer, a switch, and a speaker. the switch turns on the computer, the computer starts generating an ever-evolving version of a musical composition, and the speaker emits vibrations from the wood to fill the room with a soundscape.

compositions are each conceived as a generative system, specifically for pogłos. they intend to live in the background of our lives, setting an atmosphere, and decorating our sonic environment. it is as if the computer was quietly improvising a known repertoire.

here are some of the inspirations for the project:

  • the buddha machine, which started the initial process.
  • erik satie's vexations, for generative tendency to use repetition as a means of evolution—the same segment is repeated by the performer countless times, changing in quality throughout the duration of the piece.
  • brian eno's work with koan, in which he released albums as software.
  • listen to wikipedia, a contemporary example of music composed as a generative background.

turn it on, and let it fill the room for as long as needed.

compositions

the soundscapes loaded on the frame are played back dynamically. below are examples for the different modes of composition.

  • volume oscillation
    each sample loops continuously, fading in and out of the composition over different wave periods.
    gabor - untitled
  • polyphonic
    up to four voices can play at the same time, either randomly or in a markov chain.
    période - swirl
  • random delayed
    each sample plays back one after the other, picked randomly. a break of random duration is inserted after each sample.
    erik satie - vexations

build it yourself

the electronics

you will need:

  • an exciter speaker - (e.g. dayton 3W 4Ohm)
  • a cheap computer - (e.g. raspberry pi zero 2)
  • a power source - (e.g. 5.1V / 2.5A micro-USB power supply)
  • an SD card of at least 8Gb capacity.
  • a way for the computer to output and amplify audio - (e.g. adafruit speaker bonnet)

the frame

since the exciter speaker emits sound by vibrating the surface it is attached to, the frame will determine the sound quality in terms of dimensions, thickness and density. here is a primer on how exciters work.

for instance, a small, thick and dense surface such as a 10cm x 10cm x 10cm block of concrete will be almost silent. a large, light, and thin surface such as a 40cm x 40cm x 3cm polyester slab will resonate very well.

it's a tradeoff game, so a good compromise is a 42cm x 26cm board of 8mm thick plywood which you can cut according to this CAD file (.f3d).

finally, you will need to make all the pieces hold together. you can use superglue for the speaker ring, sticky velcro tape for the raspberry pi, and a pinch of hot glue to fasten the power cable to the surface (regular scotch tape would also work).

the software

the software is composed of three main parts:

  • a playback application
  • a wifi access point
  • a web interface

the automatic way

download the poglos disk image, unzip it and watch this video to learn how to install it on your SD card. When you are prompted to choose and operating system, select Use Custom at the bottom of the list, and select the .img file you just downloaded.

insert the SD card in the raspberry pi, power it up and, after a couple of seconds, a new wifi network should show up. connect to it using your smartphone/laptop and you should be redirected to poglos.here, the web interface where you can control composition playback. if you're not automatically redirected, you can open your browser and go to http://poglos.here directly.

the manual way

install the Raspberry Pi OS Lite (tested on Debian Bullseye). SSH into your Pi, then run the install script. at the end of the install process, reboot.

find out more on the github.
