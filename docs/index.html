pogłos
  1. an acoustic phenomenon consisting in the prolongation of sound, caused by the action of reflected waves in a closed room
  2. a multiplication of sound
  3. a reference to something, especially in someone's work

idea

pogłos is a generative sonic atmosphere contained in a wooden frame.

it is slightly bigger than an A4 piece of paper, and it consists of a a small computer, a switch, and a speaker. the switch turns on the computer, the computer starts generating an ever-evolving version of a musical composition, and the speaker emits vibrations from the wood to fill the room with a soundscape.

compositions are each conceived as a generative system, specifically for pogłos. they intend to live in the background of our lives, setting an atmosphere, and decorating our sonic environment. when they're played by the computer, it is as if it was quietly improvising a known repertoire.

here are some of the inspirations for the project:

  • the buddha machine, which started the initial process.
  • erik satie's vexations, for generative tendency to use repetition as a means of evolution—the same segment is repeated by the performer countless times, changing in quality throughout the duration of the piece.
  • brian eno's work with koan, in which he released albums as software.
  • listen to wikipedia, a contemporary example of music composed as a generative background.

turn it on, and let it fill the room for as long as needed.

sounds

the soundscapes loaded on the frame are played back dynamically. here are examples for the different modes of composition.

  • random delayed
    each sample plays back one after the other, picked randomly. a break of random duration is inserted after each sample.
    erik satie - vexations
  • polyphonic
    up to four voices can play at the same time, either randomly or in a markov chain.
    période - swirl
  • markov chain
    each sample has a defined probability to play a given sample next. this allows for a more defined structure.
    in progress
